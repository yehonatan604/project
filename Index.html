Mardi Gras New Orleans


Mardi Gras is about music, parades, picnics, floats and excitement. It's one big holiday in New Orleans!

Revelers know to wear costumes or at least dress in purple, green, and gold,
and adorn themselves with long beads caught from the floats of previous parades.

Whether they cover the wearer's eyes or whole face, masks add an element of mystery and intrigue when worn,
especially around this particular holiday in the city.
It's safe to say that masks are one of our favorite Mardi Gras traditions.

Remember: Wear the Mardi Gras colors of purple, green and gold during Carnival whenever you're not in costume.
You'll get more beads - and that's a fact no one can deny!

Carnival is a time to eat, drink and be merry before the rigorous fasting and sacrifice during Lent.
It is filled with parades, balls and other celebrations leading up to Mardi Gras, which is French for "Fat Tuesday".
Mardi Gras is always the Tuesday before Ash Wednesday.
